# Poster QA Checklist

Always inspect the rendered image. Do not rely only on file size or HTML.

## Basic Checks

- Image is sharp and matches the configured size and scale.
- Logo or brand text is visible.
- Cover headline does not overflow.
- Quick-read tags are not cramped.
- Every card is complete and readable.
- Cards do not overlap.

## Text Layout Checks

- No awkward single-character or orphan-word line breaks.
- Key industry terms are not split across lines.
- English brand names and local-language titles do not crowd each other.
- Use manual line breaks for semantic phrasing when needed.

## Footer Checks

- The footer must not carry too much information.
- Do not squeeze the main theme, usage label, selected-count label, and footer brand into one tight row.
- Main theme should usually be 1-2 lines.
- Footer should keep only essential brand or date information.
- If the bottom area feels crowded, re-layout and re-render before delivery.

## Density Checks

- The poster is for scanning, not for full explanation.
- Put explanations in the text brief.
- Keep card titles within config limits; rewrite manually when needed.

## Review Steps

1. Open the final image.
2. Scan top to bottom for visual hierarchy.
3. Zoom in to check small text readability.
4. Check configured `avoid_line_break_terms`.
5. If anything feels crowded, fix the poster before delivery.
